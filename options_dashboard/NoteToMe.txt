Regime — POSITIVE γ in green or NEGATIVE γ in red, with FADE | HIGH or TREND | LOW below

How to read it for trading:

POSITIVE γ + FADE + HIGH → strong mean-reversion environment. Sell premium, fade moves toward call wall/put wall, expect price to pin near max gamma.
NEGATIVE γ + TREND + HIGH → strong trending environment. Buy breakouts, follow momentum, expect expansion away from put wall.
Spot crossing the GEX flip line = regime change signal. Watch for it closely.

Gamma sign (POSITIVE vs NEGATIVE):
Simply the sum of all net GEX across every strike. If the total is positive, dealers are net long gamma — they have to sell when price rises and buy when price falls, which stabilizes price. If negative, dealers amplify moves.
Bias (FADE vs TREND):
Directly follows the gamma sign. Positive gamma = mean-reverting market = fade moves. Negative gamma = self-reinforcing market = follow the trend.
